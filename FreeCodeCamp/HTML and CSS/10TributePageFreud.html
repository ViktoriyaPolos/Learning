Dr. Sigmund Freud

The father of psychoanalysis

  • 1856: Sigmund Freud is born on May 6th in Freiberg, Moravia (now the Czech Republic).
  • 1873-1876: Freud begins his studies at the University of Vienna, where he initially focuses on law but later switches to medicine.
  • 1881: Freud graduates from the University of Vienna and starts working at Vienna General Hospital.
  • 1885: Freud travels to Paris to study with renowned neurologist Jean-Martin Charcot, who greatly influences his interest in hysteria and hypnosis.
  • 1886: Freud establishes a private practice in Vienna, specializing in nervous disorders.
  • 1895: Freud publishes his book "Studies on Hysteria" together with Josef Breuer, which marks his first significant work in the field of psychoanalysis.
  • 1900: Freud publishes "The Interpretation of Dreams," his most famous and influential work, in which he introduces his theories on dream analysis and the unconscious mind.
  • 1902: Freud establishes the Psychological Wednesday Society, which later becomes the Vienna Psychoanalytic Society.
  • 1909: Freud embarks on a lecture tour in the United States, where he introduces psychoanalysis to American audiences.
  • 1913-1914: Freud experiences a significant rift within the psychoanalytic movement, leading to the formation of various factions.
  • 1923: Freud is diagnosed with jaw cancer, resulting in numerous surgeries and prosthetic replacements.
  • 1938: Due to the rise of the Nazi regime, Freud and his family flee Austria and settle in London, England.
  • 1939: Freud passes away on September 23rd in London at the age of 83.
